Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun.

  • Despite being closest to the Sun, it's not the hottest planet - Venus is!
  • One Mercury day equals 176 Earth days
  • Mercury has no moons and no rings
  • Its surface is heavily cratered, similar to Earth's Moon
  • The planet is named after the Roman messenger god

Venus is Earth's closest planetary neighbor and the second planet from the Sun.

  • It's the hottest planet in our solar system, despite not being closest to the Sun
  • Venus rotates backwards compared to most other planets
  • A day on Venus is longer than its year
  • It has a toxic atmosphere of mostly carbon dioxide
  • Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to similar size

Earth is our home planet and the only known planet with life.

  • It's the only planet not named after a god or goddess
  • Earth is the only planet where water exists in all three states
  • Our planet's atmosphere protects us from meteors and radiation
  • The Earth's core is as hot as the surface of the Sun
  • 70% of the planet's surface is covered in water

Mars is known as the Red Planet and has captivated humans for centuries. Mars er kjent som "Den røde planet"

  • Mars har den største vulkanen i solsystemet, Olympus Mons.
  • Den har to måner kalt Phobos og Deimos.
  • Den røde fargen kommer fra jernoksid (rust) på overflaten.
  • Mars har sesonger slik som Jorden, men de varer dobbelt så lenge.
  • Forskere har funnet spor av tidligere vannstrømmer på Mars.
7/10

Kul farge, men litt for tørr

9/10

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and a gas giant.

  • Its Great Red Spot is a storm that's raged for over 400 years
  • Jupiter has at least 79 moons
  • It rotates faster than any other planet in the solar system
  • You could fit more than 1,300 Earths inside Jupiter
  • Its magnetic field is the strongest of all planets
6/10

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and famous for its spectacular rings.

  • Its rings are made mostly of ice and rock
  • Saturn could float in water (if there was a pool big enough)
  • It has 82 confirmed moons, including Titan
  • A year on Saturn lasts 29.5 Earth years
  • The wind speeds on Saturn can reach 1,800 km per hour
10/10

Ringene er så pene! Best dressed planet

Uranus is the seventh planet and the first discovered using a telescope.

  • It rotates on its side, unlike other planets
  • Uranus has 13 known rings
  • Its atmosphere contains "diamond rain"
  • It was originally named "George's Star"
  • One season on Uranus lasts 21 Earth years

Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet from the Sun.

  • It was discovered through mathematical predictions
  • Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system
  • It's the coldest planet in our solar system
  • One Neptune year equals 165 Earth years
  • It has a Great Dark Spot, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot
